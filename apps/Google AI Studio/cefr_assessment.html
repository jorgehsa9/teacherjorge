Texto 1: Urban Gardening

Urban gardening, the practice of growing plants in city environments, has gained significant popularity in recent years. From rooftop farms to small balcony containers, city dwellers are finding innovative ways to cultivate fresh produce and greenery. This trend is driven by several factors, including a desire for healthier, locally sourced food, an interest in sustainability, and the simple therapeutic benefits of interacting with nature. Community gardens also play a vital role, fostering social connections and providing access to fresh food in areas known as "food deserts," where supermarkets are scarce. While space limitations and soil quality can pose challenges, solutions like vertical gardening and hydroponics are making urban agriculture increasingly feasible. The greening of cities not only improves aesthetics but can also contribute to biodiversity and help mitigate the urban heat island effect.

Texto 2: The Gig Economy

The term "gig economy" refers to a labor market characterized by the prevalence of short-term contracts or freelance work, as opposed to permanent jobs. Driven largely by digital platforms that connect workers directly with clients or tasks, this model offers flexibility for workers who can often choose their own hours and projects. Examples include ride-sharing drivers, freelance writers, and graphic designers finding projects online. Proponents argue that it fosters entrepreneurship and allows individuals to diversify their income streams. However, the gig economy also faces criticism regarding job security, lack of benefits (like health insurance or paid leave), and inconsistent income. The classification of gig workers – whether they are independent contractors or employees – is a contentious legal issue in many countries, impacting their rights and protections. Balancing the flexibility desired by many workers with the need for fair labor standards remains a central challenge.
